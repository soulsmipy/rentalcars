Frequently Asked Questions for India

What documents do I need to rent a car in India?

To rent a car in India, you'll need:

  • A valid Indian driving license or International Driving Permit (for foreign visitors)
  • Passport or government-issued ID proof
  • Credit card in the renter's name for security deposit
  • Address proof if renting for more than 30 days
What is the minimum age to rent a car in India?

The minimum age to rent a car in India is 21 years. However:

  • Some premium/luxury cars may require the driver to be at least 25 years old
  • Young driver fees may apply for renters under 25
  • Additional insurance may be required for drivers under 25
Can I take the rental car from one city to another in India?

Yes, but with some conditions:

  • Most rentals allow inter-city travel within India
  • Some states may require additional documentation
  • One-way rentals between cities may have additional fees
  • Always inform the rental company about your travel plans
  • Certain border areas may have restrictions
What fuel policy is common for car rentals in India?

Most car rentals in India follow these fuel policies:

  • Full-to-Full: You receive the car with a full tank and must return it full
  • Pre-paid Fuel: You pay for a full tank upfront and can return the car at any fuel level
  • Fuel charges are typically higher than local pump prices if you don't return the car full
  • Diesel vehicles are common and often more economical for long distances
Is it safe to drive a rental car in India?

Driving in India can be challenging but safe if you follow these tips:

  • Familiarize yourself with local traffic rules and driving customs
  • Consider hiring a driver if you're not comfortable with Indian road conditions
  • Stick to main highways and avoid night driving in rural areas
  • Always carry your documents and emergency contact numbers
  • Consider comprehensive insurance coverage
  • Use GPS navigation to avoid getting lost
What insurance coverage is included with my rental in India?

Standard rental car insurance in India typically includes:

  • Third-party liability coverage (mandatory by law)
  • Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) with an excess/deductible amount
  • Theft Protection with an excess/deductible amount
  • Additional options available:
    • Zero Depreciation Cover (reduces your liability)
    • Personal Accident Insurance
    • Roadside Assistance

Always read the terms carefully and consider additional coverage based on your needs.
